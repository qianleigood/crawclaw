# Style and Soul Guide

Use this reference when the text is technically clean but still feels sterile, formulaic, or obviously machine-generated.

## Signs of lifeless writing

- every sentence has similar rhythm
- no viewpoint or emotional temperature
- no uncertainty, tension, or personal perspective where appropriate
- reads like a policy memo or press release even when it should feel human

## How to add humanity

- allow viewpoint, not just neutral reporting
- vary sentence length and cadence
- acknowledge complexity rather than flatten it
- use first person when the context genuinely benefits from it
- allow a small amount of asymmetry or messiness when tone permits
- replace abstract emotion with concrete feeling or consequence

## Warning

Humanizing does not mean fabricating facts, citations, or experience.
